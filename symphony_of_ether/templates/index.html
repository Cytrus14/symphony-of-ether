Bode

The Bode Galaxy, also known as Messier 81 (M81), is a spiral galaxy located in the constellation Ursa Major. It is one of the brightest and best-known spiral galaxies in the night sky. This galaxy has distinct spiral arms and is approximately 11.8 million light-years away. M81 is an object frequently observed by astronomers and is an important object for studying the structure and evolution of spiral galaxies.

Angelfish Cluster

The galaxy M71, also known as Messier 71, is a globular cluster located in the constellation Sagittarius in the night sky. It was discovered in 1746 by Philippe Loys de Chéseaux and later cataloged by Charles Messier in 1780. M71 is one of many globular clusters found in our Galaxy, and a large number of older stars cluster together to form a beautiful and dense cosmic cluster.

The galaxy M78 is a reflection nebula located in the constellation Orion. It is a bright and easily visible nebula whose blue light comes from the diffuse reflection of the light of young, hot stars inside it. M78 is a popular object in amateur astronomy and is part of the Orion nebula complex, full of fascinating cosmic features.

Crab Nebula

The M1 galaxy, also known as Messier 1 or the Crab Nebula, is a supernova remnant discovered in 1054 by Chinese and Arab astronomers. It is a planetary nebula located in the constellation Taurus and is one of the most famous objects of this type in the sky. The Crab Nebula is the subject of intense scientific research and is visible as a bright spot of space in the night sky.
